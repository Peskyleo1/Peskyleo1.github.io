Reflective Surface
Unit: Off / On

Type: Input

Usage:
Activate if you're at the beach or on snow.

Description:
Albedo is the measure of the diffuse reflection of solar radiation. It is measured on a scale from 0 (black body) to 1 (reflects all incident radiation).

Snow albedo is highly variable, ranging from as high as 0.9 for freshly fallen snow, to about 0.4 for melting snow, and as low as 0.2 for dirty snow.

Water reflects light very differently from typical terrestrial materials. Although the reflectivity of water is very low at low and medium angles of incident light, it becomes very high at high angles of incident light.

Description:
The time displayed is the maximum theoretical time you can stay in the sun. This is calculated with a mathematical formula. There is a difference between theory and practice though. In theory with some creams you may be able to stay in the sun for extended periods. In practice there are some effects such as sweat, humidity, diving in water, and friction against objects which may render long theoretical times not really useful in practice. Keep this in mind as a general rule of thumb:
